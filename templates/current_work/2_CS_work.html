Standard Cox and Strack method requires additional time-consuming steps required for measuring the contact resistivity of samples with full-area coated conductive layers. The reason lies in the analytical formulation of the 'spreading resistance' in classical formulation. In this work, we propose a simple and time-efficient formulation for 'spreading resistance' that is calculated in a matter of miliseconds, therefore, improving the original method.

To verify the proposed formulation we performed numerical and experimental analysis. For numerical analysis, we have tracked the relative error between derived formula and finite-element method simulations (using COMSOL). We found an acceptable error of less than 30% for edge cases and of less than 3% for common cases.

Then we compared the extracted contact resistiviy values of experimental samples using our formulation and numerical approach. We obtained a good agreement the two approaches.
