When contact resistivity is measured using full-area samples, it is usually not corrected in any way. In this work we found that it can be significantly overestimated due to current spreading inside the metal layer. To adjust for that overestimation, we analytically solved for resistance values of the full-area structures taking into account the current spreading in metal layer. This provided a way of correcting measured contact resistivity and measure more accurate values.
We tested the derived formulation against finite-element simulations (performed in COMSOL) and found that it has a good accuracy (with relative error being smaller than 2%). Then using proposed formulation we corrected the resistivity values of the fabricated experimental samples. The bottomline of this study suggests that contact resistivity can be significantly overestimated for relatively thin metal layers, and therefore should be corrected to account for limited current spreading.
